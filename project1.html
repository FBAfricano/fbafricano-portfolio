Predicting Algal Bloom Occurrences

← Back to Portfolio

About this project

BloomSentry is the thesis project I developed with my group. My main role was to construct the user interface from the ground up. This interface allows users, such as environmental agencies and researchers, to easily see and understand the system's predictions about future harmful algal bloom (HABs) events.

The system pulls environmental data, processes it through a machine learning model, and then visualizes the risk levels on an interactive map, helping to mitigate the environmental and economic impact of HABs.

Features & Functions

  • ◆ Monthly Forecasting: Provides a forward-looking predictive outlook on the occurrence and severity of algal bloom indicators.
  • ◆ Indicator Prediction: Forecasts crucial parameters such as Chlorophyll-a concentration, Phytoplankton Count, Nitrogen levels, and Phosphate concentration.
  • ◆ Bloom Location: Utilizes station data, wind speed, and wind direction to predict the potential distribution of an algal bloom.
  • ◆ Optimized Model: Uses a Gradient Boosting Regressor fine-tuned with BayesSearchCV to achieve peak predictive performance.
